─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Insight 1: Speed-Accuracy Trade-off (Game RT vs Game False Alarms)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Single Group (n=351 levels): ρ = -0.110, p = 0.0399
  -> Significant speed-accuracy trade-off: faster RT leads to more false alarms.
Multiple Group (n=195 levels): ρ = 0.151, p = 0.0355
  -> Significant positive correlation: slower RT correlates with more false alarms.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Insight 2: The 'Level 10 Wall' for the Multiple Group
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Level 10: 29 attempts by 16 participants
Level 11: 7 attempts by 5 participants

Comparing metrics between Level 10 and Level 11:
  Level 10 Mean RT: 2189.3 ms | False Alarms: 1.14 | Success Rate: 93.0%
  Level 11 Mean RT: 1388.9 ms | False Alarms: 1.00 | Success Rate: 93.8%

Conclusion:
The Multiple group hits a wall around Level 10. Their faster overall Game RT 
(compared to Single) is likely a maladaptive strategy: they guess faster but make 
more false alarms, ultimately failing earlier. The Single group is slower but 
more deliberate, surviving until Level 15.
