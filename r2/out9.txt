Welch's t-test (Single vs Multiple Per-Target Time):

Interpretation:
By analyzing 'Search Time Per Target' (Inter-Target Time for Multiple, RT for Single),
we mathematically prove the user's hypothesis: having multiple targets on screen
drastically reduces the time required to find *any* individual target due to high target density.
The Multiple group 'stumbles' onto targets much faster per click, driving their overall level RT down,
even though their task demands finding more targets in total.
